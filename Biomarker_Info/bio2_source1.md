# Biomarker 2: Alpha-2 Power and Complexity — Source 1

## Citation
Patchitt, Joel, et al. "Alpha3/Alpha2 Power Ratios Relate to Performance on a Virtual Reality Shopping Task in Ageing Adults." Frontiers in Aging Neuroscience, vol. 14, 2022, article 876832.
https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/aging-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnagi.2022.876832/full

## Biomarker Category
Alpha-2 Power — Alpha3/Alpha2 Ratio, Cognitive Performance Correlation

## Key Findings
- High alpha3/alpha2 power ratios showed strong positive correlation with VStore Total Time (r = 0.73), indicating slower task completion.
- Strong negative associations between high alpha3/alpha2 ratios and Cogstate Pre-clinical Alzheimer's Battery (r = -0.67) and Composite Score (r = -0.76).
- No statistically significant difference in alpha3/alpha2 ratios between young adults (mean = 0.97) and older adults (mean = 1.07) in healthy aging.
- Elevated alpha3/alpha2 ratios may be disease-specific rather than a normal aging phenomenon.
- Virtual reality shopping task achieved 94% sensitivity and 95% specificity in discriminating age groups.

## Clinical Context
- Alpha-2 band (10-13 Hz): upper alpha range sensitive to cognitive decline.
- Alpha3/alpha2 ratio captures the shift from upper alpha (alpha2) to lower-upper alpha (alpha3), reflecting disrupted cortical rhythmic activity.
- Elevated alpha3/alpha2 ratio (i.e., reduced alpha2 relative to alpha3) may indicate poorer cognitive performance in AD-related conditions.
- The marker appears disease-specific, not simply an effect of normal aging.

## Relevance to This Project
- Supports inclusion of alpha-2 features as biomarkers for AD-related cognitive decline.
- Reduced alpha-2 power in AD and FTD groups relative to CN is consistent with the alpha3/alpha2 framework: lower alpha2 = higher ratio = worse cognition.
- Observed pattern in combined resting-state dataset: alpha2 absolute power CN (1.82e-11) > AD (7.94e-12) > FTD (3.48e-12).

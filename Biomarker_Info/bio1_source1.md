# Biomarker 1: Spectral Power Ratios — Source 1

## Citation
Moretti, Davide V. "Theta and Alpha EEG Frequency Interplay in Subjects with Mild Cognitive Impairment: Evidence from EEG, MRI, and SPECT Brain Modifications." Frontiers in Aging Neuroscience, vol. 7, 2015, article 31.
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4396516/

## Biomarker Category
Spectral Power Ratios — Theta/Alpha Ratio, Alpha3/Alpha2 Ratio

## Key Findings
- Higher alpha3/alpha2 power ratio is significantly associated with increased risk of MCI converting to Alzheimer's disease (p=0.0001).
- Elevated alpha3/alpha2 ratio correlates with both increased alpha3 (p=0.001) and decreased alpha2 (p=0.0001) power.
- A positive correlation was found between elevated alpha3/alpha2 ratio and increased theta frequency power in high-risk MCI patients.
- Positive correlation between hippocampal perfusion and theta rhythm specifically in the AD high-risk group.
- High alpha3/alpha2 patients showed reduced left hippocampal volumes (p<0.001).
- Temporo-parietal cortical thinning (precuneus, supramarginal gyrus) was associated with higher alpha3/alpha2 values.
- Constant trend toward lower cerebral blood perfusion in the high-risk group.

## Clinical Context
- EEG slowing (increased theta, decreased alpha) is one of the most consistently reported quantitative EEG changes in AD and MCI.
- Theta/alpha ratio captures this slowing pattern: higher values indicate more dementia-like EEG activity.
- The alpha3/alpha2 ratio specifically captures the shift from upper alpha (alpha2, 10-13 Hz) to lower-upper alpha (alpha3, 13+ Hz), which reflects thalamocortical rhythm disruption.

## Relevance to This Project
- Supports use of theta/alpha ratio as a spectral biomarker for dementia-related EEG slowing.
- Observed pattern in combined resting-state dataset: AD > FTD > CN for theta/alpha ratio.
- Higher theta/alpha ratio in AD (2.95) vs FTD (2.69) vs CN (1.99) is consistent with this paper's findings.

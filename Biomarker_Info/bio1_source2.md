# Biomarker 1: Spectral Power Ratios — Source 2

## Citation
Papaliagkas, Vasileios. "The Role of Quantitative EEG in the Diagnosis of Alzheimer's Disease." Diagnostics, vol. 15, no. 15, 2025, article 1965.
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12346855/

## Biomarker Category
Spectral Power Ratios — DTABR, Theta/Alpha Ratio, Band Power Changes

## Key Findings
- AD is associated with significant alpha power reductions primarily in occipital, parietal, and posterior temporal regions, correlating with cognitive deterioration severity.
- Increased theta activity in medial temporal and frontal regions associates with memory deficits in AD.
- AD demonstrates higher relative power in the delta frequency band.
- Significant reductions in beta and gamma power in prefrontal and temporal regions impair attention and executive function.
- Theta/alpha ratio shows elevated values in AD, with potential to forecast MCI-to-AD transition.
- Theta/alpha ratio exceeds diagnostic accuracy of individual power measures alone.

## Diagnostic Accuracy from Referenced Studies
- Alpha/theta ratio: sensitivity = 73%, specificity = 82% (Schmidt et al., 2013)
- Spectral and wavelet analysis: sensitivity = 92-96%, specificity = 84-92% (Bairagi, 2018)
- MCI vs. normal controls: sensitivity = 98.32%, specificity = 99.66% (Siuly et al.)
- Combined measures: accuracy = 89.1% in AD vs. controls

## Clinical Context
- Quantitative EEG (qEEG) biomarkers for AD include: slowing of dominant frequency, altered rhythmic activity, reduced complexity, and disrupted connectivity.
- DTABR = (delta + theta) / (alpha + beta): captures broad EEG slowing by combining increased slow-wave activity with reduced faster activity.
- Higher DTABR values indicate stronger dementia-like slowing.

## Relevance to This Project
- Supports use of both DTABR and theta/alpha ratio as spectral biomarkers for AD and FTD detection.
- Observed pattern in combined resting-state dataset: DTABR AD (31.2) ≈ FTD (30.1) > CN (23.1).
- Consistent with the paper's report of increased delta/theta and decreased alpha/beta in AD.

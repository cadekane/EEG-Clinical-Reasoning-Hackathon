# Biomarker 3: EEG Microstates — Source 2

## Citation
Chang, Jinwon. "EEG Microstate Differences Between Alzheimer's Disease, Frontotemporal Dementia, and Healthy Controls Using 4 and 7 Clustering Classes with a Ratio Approach." Medicina, vol. 61, no. 11, 2025, article 1917.
https://www.mdpi.com/1648-9144/61/11/1917

## Biomarker Category
EEG Microstates — Coverage, Duration, Occurrence, MMSE Correlation, AD/FTD/CN Differentiation

## Key Findings
- Four-class microstate analysis showed that temporal parameters of microstates B and D differentiated healthy controls from dementia groups (AD and FTD).
- Ratio-normalized microstate parameters improved group discrimination compared to raw microstate parameters.
- Ratio-normalized microstate parameters were associated with Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE) scores, indicating clinical relevance.
- Both 4-class and 7-class microstate clustering were evaluated; ratio approach improved separation across diagnostic groups.
- Microstate B and D temporal features (duration, occurrence, coverage) were most discriminative between dementia and control groups.

## Clinical Context
- Microstates represent short, recurring topographic brain states corresponding to large-scale brain network activations.
- AD and FTD both show microstate disorganization, but the pattern of affected microstates may differ between conditions.
- MMSE (Mini-Mental State Examination): 0-30 scale of cognitive function; lower scores indicate greater impairment.
- Ratio-normalized microstate parameters reduce the effect of overall signal amplitude variability across subjects.
- FTD affects frontal and temporal networks differently than AD, which may be reflected in distinct microstate patterns.

## Relevance to This Project
- Directly supports inclusion of microstate B coverage as primary microstate biomarker for AD/FTD/CN separation.
- Provides evidence that microstate temporal parameters correlate with MMSE, linking EEG microstate changes to clinical severity.
- Observed pattern in combined resting-state dataset: microstate B coverage FTD (0.256) ≈ AD (0.257) > CN (0.236).

# Biomarker 2: Alpha-2 Power and Complexity — Source 2

## Citation
Moretti, Davide V. "Conversion of Mild Cognitive Impairment Patients in Alzheimer's Disease: Prognostic Value of Alpha3/Alpha2 Electroencephalographic Rhythms Power Ratio." Alzheimer's Research & Therapy, vol. 7, 2015, article 80.
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4696332/

## Biomarker Category
Alpha-2 Power — Alpha3/Alpha2 Ratio, MCI-to-AD Conversion, Structural Brain Changes

## Key Findings
- MCI patients with higher alpha3/alpha2 ratios (>1.17) showed significantly greater likelihood of converting to AD within three years.
- Discriminant analysis achieved 88.3% correct classification accuracy using theta/gamma and alpha3/alpha2 ratios as primary discriminating variables.
- High alpha3/alpha2 group showed greater memory impairment correlated with cortical atrophy and lower perfusion in temporo-parietal cortex.
- Cortical thinning in bilateral superior temporal, supramarginal, and precuneus regions; total gray matter reduction of 471 mm² compared to low-ratio groups.
- Left hippocampal volumes significantly lower (p=0.001) in AD-high risk patients.
- Thalamocortical and cortico-cortical components interact in the generation of cortical alpha rhythms.

## Clinical Context
- Alpha3/alpha2 ratio: higher ratio means less alpha2 (upper alpha) and more alpha3 (lower-upper alpha), indicating disrupted cortical rhythmic activity.
- Alpha-2 (10-13 Hz) reduction is associated with structural brain changes including cortical thinning and hippocampal atrophy.
- Alpha-2 absolute power can vary across individuals due to biological differences (electrode impedance, skull thickness, hair), so relative power is more stable.
- Alpha-2 relative power normalizes by total band power, reducing inter-subject variability.

## Relevance to This Project
- Supports use of alpha-2 absolute and relative power as biomarkers for AD detection.
- Justifies treating alpha2_rel as more stable than alpha2_abs for group comparison.
- Observed pattern: alpha2 relative power CN (0.0376) > AD (0.0284) ≈ FTD (0.0247), consistent with reduced alpha-2 in dementia groups.
